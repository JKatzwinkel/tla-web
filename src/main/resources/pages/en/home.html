Welcome to the Thesaurus Linguae Aegyptiae (TLA)!
Immerse yourself into a wealth of Egyptian texts.

You will find a great variety of writings, from terse labels up to extensive narratives, produced by Egyptian scribes and recorded on the walls of temples and tombs, on stelae, papyrus scrolls and ostraca, on grave goods, tools, vessels and instruments.

Detect the earliest written religious texts of mankind, read ancient letters, tales, travel guides to the netherworld, or an early state treaty, try to savvy ancient mathematics, astronomy and magical practices, find out the therapeutical measures taken by Egyptian physicians.

The Egyptian-Coptic language is the human language with the longest documented lifetime, spanning over more than 4,000 years from the invention of hieroglyphic script ca. 3,000 BCE up to its extinction at around 1,400 CE. Its vocabulary reflects the knowledge and worldviews of one of the formative cultures of the ancient world.

The Thesaurus Linguae Aegyptiae (TLA) provides the worldwide largest electronic corpus of Egyptian full text data transcribed from hieroglyphic, hieratic, and Demotic records.

The TLA offers two core tools for searching the Egyptian-Coptic text world and language, the dictionary and the text corpus.
